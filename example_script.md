slide 4: Michelson believed in the ether until he died in 1931. In the late 1800s, physicists believed that light waves needed a medium to travel through, just as sound travels through air or ripples travel through water. They called this invisible substance the luminiferous ether. Einstein wasn't even sure he'd read the experiment. Instead of trying to explain why the ether wind couldn't be detected, Einstein started with a radical assumption: The ether doesn't exist. He proposed two simple postulates that changed everything.
